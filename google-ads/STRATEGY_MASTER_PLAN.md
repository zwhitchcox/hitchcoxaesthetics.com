## The 4-Pillar Campaign Structure

### 1. 🛡️ The Brand Shield (Campaign: `01-Search-Brand`)

**What it is:** A Search campaign bidding strictly on your name: "Sarah Hitchcox
Aesthetics." **Why you need it:** Competitors _will_ bid on your name. If
someone searches for you and sees a competitor's ad first, you lose a warm lead.
Brand clicks are incredibly cheap (usually < $1.00), and they have the highest
conversion rate. **Budget:** $5 - $10 / day. (It will only spend when people
specifically search for you).

### 2. 🎯 High-Intent Search (Campaign: `02-Search-NonBrand`)

**What it is:** This is the core "bread and butter." We capture people at the
exact moment they search for "Botox near me" or "Lip fillers Knoxville." **Why
you need it:** This captures the "Bottom of the Funnel." I have broken this into
5 specific Ad Groups (Botox, Fillers, Laser, Microneedling, GLP-1).
**Strategy:** We use Exact Match `[bracket]` and Phrase Match `"quotes"`
keywords _only_. No broad match, which wastes money. **Budget:** ~60% of your
total budget.

### 3. 🚀 The AI Engine (Campaign: `03-Performance-Max`)

**What it is:** Performance Max (PMax) replaced Local Campaigns. It uses your
Google Business Profile (GBP) to show ads on Google Maps, YouTube, Gmail, and
the Display Network. **Why you need it:** In 2026, PMax is how local MedSpas
win. When someone searches "MedSpa near me" on Google Maps, PMax ensures your
clinic pin shows up first. By feeding PMax "Audience Signals" (like your past
patient email list and competitor URLs), Google's AI finds people who _look_
like your best patients but haven't searched for you yet. **Budget:** ~25% of
your total budget.

### 4. 🧲 The Net (Campaign: `04-Demand-Gen-Retargeting`)

**What it is:** Demand Gen campaigns place highly visual image/video ads on
YouTube Shorts, Google Discover, and Gmail. **Why you need it:** Aesthetic
procedures are expensive. People often visit your site, look at pricing, and
leave to think about it. Demand Gen _retargets_ those website visitors for 30-90
days, staying top-of-mind until they are ready to book. It is the cheapest way
to get high-value conversions. **Budget:** ~10% to 15% of your total budget.

## Advanced 2026 Tactics You Need to Implement

### 1. Value-Based Bidding (VBB)

Do not treat all conversions equally. A Botox consultation is not worth the same
to your business as a 6-month Semaglutide program.

- In Google Ads > Conversions, assign an **estimated dollar value** to your lead
  forms.
- E.g., General Consult = $100. Semaglutide Consult = $500.
- Once you have 30+ conversions in a month, switch your Search campaign bid
  strategy from _Maximize Conversions_ to **Maximize Conversion Value (tROAS)**.
  Google's algorithm will start hunting for the "whale" clients rather than just
  the cheapest clicks.

### 2. Offline Conversion Tracking (OCT)

Since you use CallRail and Google Analytics, ensure you are tracking which leads
actually _show up_ and _pay_. If a Google Ad generates 10 leads, but 8 are
unqualified, Google's AI will think it did a good job and find _more_
unqualified people. By feeding back the actual closed sales data (via
HubSpot/Jane App/CallRail integration), you train the AI to find high-paying
patients.

### 3. LegitScript Certification

Because you offer Medical Weight Loss (GLP-1s) and Botox, Google's Healthcare
Policy bots will inevitably flag your account at some point for "Prescription
Drugs." **The Pro Move:** Apply for LegitScript Certification for your website.
It costs a fee, but once you provide the certificate to Google Ads, your account
gets whitelisted. Your ads will never get paused again, while your competitors
deal with constant disapprovals.

### 4. Image Formatting for AI

Google's algorithms heavily favor accounts that provide every required asset
size. Take your best clinic interior shots and "After" patient photos and use a
free tool like Canva to crop them into:

- **Square (1:1)**
- **Landscape (1.91:1)**
- **Portrait (4:5)** - _Crucial for YouTube Shorts / mobile visibility._

Upload all of these into the Google Ads Asset Library.
